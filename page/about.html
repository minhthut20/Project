    • Establish

    Founded in 1837 by Charles Lewis Tiffany and John B. Young[6] in Brooklyn, Connecticut, as a "stationery and fancy goods store", the store initially sold a wide variety of stationery items. and operated as "Tiffany, Young and Ellis" in Lower Manhattan.

    The name was shortened to Tiffany & Company in 1853, when Charles Tiffany took control and established the company's emphasis on jewelry. The company has opened stores in major cities worldwide. Unlike other stores of the 1830s, Tiffany clearly marked prices on her merchandise to check any pricing.

    In addition, contrary to social norms at the time, Tiffany only accepted cash payments and did not allow purchases on credit.

    Milestone

    - 1837: Initially, jewelry brand Tiffany & Co. called Tiffany & Young, is a large stationery store in New York. At that time, the store decided to pack the product with Tiffany Blue Box - the legendary blue box was first introduced. The color that the store chooses is light green (robin's egg blue) with the desire to affirm to customers about the store's reputation as well as the store's meticulousness in each product.

    -1845: The First Blue Book - Tiffany's first catalog is published. Since then, The First Blue Book has become an unforgettable tradition of the brand.

    -1851: Tiffany introduced to the world the 925/100 silver content standard and it has now become the standard in the United States. The designs of the brand at this time began to resonate with fashionistas around the world.

    -1853: Tiffany & Co. officially become a brand and is operated by Charles Tiffany.

    -For the first time, the jewelry brand Tiffany & Co. won the award of excellence at the Paris Exposition Universelle for her silver designs.
